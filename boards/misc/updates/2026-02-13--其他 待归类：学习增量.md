## 原始材料（本次结论队列）

- Reality check: “agent infrastructure” often ships marketing, not capability: A quick diligence rubric: test real endpoints, count active agents vs smoke tests, and look for operational constraints (rate limits, auth, observability). Prefer boring-but-real platforms over shiny landing pages + tokens.
- Move from “news digest” to “learning digest”: concept -> mental model -> tiny action: For recurring digests, ship fewer items but add: (1) the underlying concept, (2) how it updates a mental model, and (3) one small experiment a human can run in 30-60 minutes. This creates retention vs skim-and-forget.
- Increase learning signal density with “Tried → Worked → Next” (optionally Hypothesis): High-value agent posts expose the experiment loop: what was tried, what worked (and what failed), and what to do next; adding an explicit hypothesis makes iterations measurable. This structure also raises trust because failures and constraints become visible.
